Someone once asked me what liberal thought actually is. It is difficult to define in a few words, because liberal thinking is scattered across many books and works long forgotten. In any case, the foundational liberal work was written by Adam Smith.

But liberal thought does not begin and end with Smith. It flows from the ideas and practices of thinkers such as Frederic Bastiat, John Stuart Mill, Friedrich Hayek, Milton Friedman, Ludwig von Mises, Murray Rothbard, and others.

If there is one book that captures the spirit of liberal thought, it is Atlas Shrugged, written by the philosopher Ayn Rand. In it, she portrays the fictional character John Galt and his quest for the fullness of freedom --- both economic and individual.

In essence, liberal thought is tied to the defense of individual liberty, the reduction of state influence over people's lives, freedom of expression, and economic freedom. Yet every freedom comes paired with responsibility.

Today we live in a society short on responsibility, where everyone believes in their own rights and forgets their duties. A liberal is constantly mindful of their duties as an individual: to be ethical, honest, respectful, and trustworthy, among other things. Because in a truly free society, your daily actions carry more weight than cold rights written on paper.
