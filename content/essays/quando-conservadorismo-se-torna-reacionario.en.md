When we speak of conservative thought, something old invariably comes to mind — and that is no surprise. This school of thinking is grounded in tradition, Western values, the defense of the family, an appreciation for the sacred, and more. In a postmodern society, holding traditional values in high regard sounds strange. And no wonder.

We live today among a multitude of genders, tastes, flavors, and desires. Nothing is permanent; everything is mutable. Such is life in a society that is open to all things — yet not libertine, according to conservatives. However, the defense of conservative thought is gradually giving way to reactionary thinking: a yearning to return to a past buried in the dust of oblivion.

It is no coincidence that we see daily protests against individual freedoms, coming from the very people who ought to defend their worldview without interfering in the views of others. An ideological patrol has emerged, laden with idiosyncrasies and determined to tear down anyone who does not follow its agenda. There is a frenzied search for enemies, and whoever offers even a pointed critique is treated as a foe rather than an intellectual adversary.

Unless a deep reflection takes place in the way these groups think — an effort to understand those who are different — we will end up in the same era when people wore brown shirts and smashed windows in the name of a past that will never return.
