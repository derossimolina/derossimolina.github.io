We live in the 21st century, yet we remain chained to the past. We do not believe in change, in technology, or in the possibility of turning our city into a hub of innovation in governance. Our politics is built on precepts from the 20th century, loaded with idiosyncrasies that lack any scientific backing. We are in the age of data science, artificial intelligence, and liquid democracy, yet in Caxias we debate the minutiae of whether to spend more or less on healthcare or education.

What good is arguing over spending if we are still trapped in outdated models? While students discuss careers as YouTubers, teachers barely have access to the internet. While telemedicine is discussed in doctors' offices, patients in the public system wait months for a bottle of basic medication.

Today we do not have the figure of the public manager, someone focused on the strategic direction of the municipality. We have only the "public firefighter" --- the one who puts out administrative fires, whether sparked by circumstance or by accusations in the press.

Civic renewal movements such as Livres, Agora, Acredito, and RenovaBR are developing political leaders for this century's challenges: social inequality, urban mobility, access to public services, wealth creation, regulatory flexibility, and job generation. These movements are grounded in scientific studies and data analysis, with feasible proposals for the development of responsible public policies --- with clear, transparent objectives for the entire community. No guesswork, no bravado.

This is the kind of politics Caxias needs. Rooted in reliable information, built on study and dedication, to move beyond easy promises and the simplistic label that our city cannot be better. With political renewal, we can build a Caxias today with its eyes on tomorrow.
