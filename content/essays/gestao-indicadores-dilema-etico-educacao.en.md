State-level public education management faces a critical dilemma: the conflict between teaching integrity and the pursuit of favorable approval rates. Across many contexts, there is systemic pressure on faculty to maintain positive statistical indicators, often at the expense of pedagogical reality and professional autonomy.

A recent case illustrates this administrative dysfunction. Teachers — particularly those on temporary contracts — are urged by school administrators to assign grades to students who never completed the required assessments. When these teachers uphold technical rigor and professional ethics, they face an environment of institutional pressure that frequently makes their continued employment untenable, culminating in a departure that, though formally requested, reflects a veiled form of coercion. Situations like these reveal how the vulnerability of precarious employment can be weaponized to silence technical and ethical dissent.

This phenomenon, widely debated in public policy circles, masks the educational crisis by turning schools into bureaucratic approval-processing units. For the administrative structure, the spreadsheet is filled and the target is met; for society, real learning is neglected. The precariousness of temporary contracts aggravates the picture, since job instability strips teachers of the basic safeguards they need to exercise their pedagogical authority.

Beyond the ethical toll, recurring failures in the administrative workflow surrounding terminations — such as errors in processing final pay and severance obligations — reveal governance deficiencies in the state's education system. The absence of efficient and transparent administrative support compromises the dignity of workers and the continuity of public service.

We must ask whether Public Administration should serve to embellish statistical data or to genuinely educate citizens. These are not isolated incidents but symptoms of a management model that prioritizes metrics over ethics, punishing the professionals who choose the path of academic integrity.
