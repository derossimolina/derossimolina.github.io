Driven by the engine of the "invisible hand," the economy cannot stop. That is the virtue of the free market, fueled by commercial relations among various actors, both public and private. To strip away this freedom is to destroy a fundamental pillar of any minimally free society. Yet a pandemic appeared on the path, forcing everyone to slow the economy to the point of paralyzing nearly all activity. There is no other remedy to prevent infections. Without resorting to guesswork, I echo the WHO's own recommendation --- social isolation --- as the only way to ease the pressure on healthcare systems.

Unfortunately, there is no end date, no holiday to mark on the calendar. Everything depends on the number of infections, recoveries, severe cases, and vaccines. But it takes time --- a great deal of time for those accustomed to doing business at the speed of a click. According to researchers, vaccines could begin distribution in twelve months, meaning around mid-2021.

Will we be locked down until then? Of course not. But the decision to relax social isolation measures does not depend on political meetings or open letters. It depends on analysis, data, and above all the patient temperament of scientists. Moreover, the return must be gradual, respecting the needs of the healthcare system to prevent its collapse.

Will any of this be good for the economy? Never. Companies will go bankrupt, people will be laid off, inflation will rise, debts will mount, and hunger and poverty will deepen. This is where governments must step in with income transfer measures --- not timid or sporadic ones. There is a need for a universal basic income for all Brazilians, because one way or another, we have all been affected. This must be combined with low-interest credit for micro and small businesses. But it will not come cheap. Government spending will have to be cut for many years after the storm.

Yet we will be strong again in the medium term, with profound social changes but greater economic independence. Governments will lack resources in the future, and only we --- individually and collectively --- will be able to safeguard our tomorrow. We will, at last, be freer.
