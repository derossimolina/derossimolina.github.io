I wake up in the middle of the night with a bad feeling, not exclusive to me, but shared by everyone. Why are our lives so gray? Sometimes I remember when our lives were so colorful, when the sunshine shone everywhere. We smiled more, laughed more, and had ---maybe a few ---friends. We talked more, thought more, and had some hope.

Nowadays everything is so gray. Everywhere I look, whenever I talk to someone, everyone is sorrowful, sad, and hopeless. Why?

I remember, 20 years ago, my hometown was crowded with people walking on the streets. Worried about their personal lives, but with hope for the future. I saw colors in everything I looked at. No one said "I'm waiting for the meteor" or "I prefer an atomic bomb." Children laughed at school, and at work we helped each other.

At the bus stop we talked about the weather, politics, or supermarket prices. But it was so vivid, so warm, so human. Now at the bus stop I see people with suffering faces, due to work or life's problems; some are sick or don't have money to buy food.

Why did this happen to us?

When will we feel the sunshine shining on our heads again?

Is this the future, a place controlled by technology, a cyberpunk dystopia between Blade Runner and Elysium?

Or worse than the movies?

When will this gray color disappear from our lives?
