The science of Administration is typically treated as a positivist field — and for good reason. The bulk of studies, research, and scientific work in the area views the world through that lens, regarding truth as something distant and amenable to detached observation.

Over the years, however, the discipline has been moving in new directions, more attuned to the concerns of people in the twenty-first century. Research in sustainability, circular economy, governance, and social impact reveals a current far more connected to contemporary life.

Yet in many organizations, managerial models based on structures of domination persist, blocking the emancipation of workers and oppressing people on the basis of race, gender, and origin. This kind of superstructure within organizations stifles discussion of fundamental issues such as internal democracy, colonial and decolonial struggles, simulation and simulacra, among others.

This critical perspective on organizations aims to unmask everything that prevents individuals from becoming fully free in all their subjectivity. It offers a new lens — harsher, no doubt, but honest — on what millions upon millions of people endure every day, shining light on the dark side of enterprises, both public and private, in order to foster a fairer and more equal working environment for everyone, from employee to employer.

This perspective draws on the tenets of Critical Theory from the Frankfurt School, but also on postmodernism, feminism, Queer Theory, post-Marxism, Anti-colonial Studies, and Urban Studies — demonstrating that research in Administration extends far beyond neoliberal or capitalist precepts.
