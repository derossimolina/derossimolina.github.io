Before you ask — this is not an article about physics. Quite the opposite. Last Sunday, the series finale of *The Big Bang Theory* aired in Brazil: a sitcom featuring anything but conventional characters, all of them geeks — once known simply as nerds.

The show truly popularized geek culture and everything that orbits that world. From comic books to computing, from science fiction to physics, from mathematics to cosplay — a universe that before the series had been restricted to a handful of enthusiasts was unveiled to millions of people around the globe.

Yet the show's greatest contribution, even as a comedy, was demonstrating that we need more science in our lives. Nothing in the world would exist without the dedication and effort of scientists across every field of knowledge, working to unravel the mysteries that haunt humanity.

We need more science, more research, more nerds and geeks. We need more "Big Bangs."
