Exception. The word says it all: something that departs from the conventional, from normality. The saying "every rule has its exception" makes the term perfectly clear. Throughout our history, we have lived through episodes of exception — moments when the rule ceased to be the rule.

In times of crisis, we typically crave exceptions. It is a human impulse to override the standing rules in order to change the status quo. Being human, we assign the task of changing the rules to others, never to ourselves. No one likes it when someone else breaks the rules.

Yet we forget the old saying. When we stop following the rules, we create exceptions. We leave familiar ground and venture into terrain where nothing is certain. Unable to see tomorrow, we become hostages of today. We do not plan, we do not experiment with innovation, we do not advance. We stand still, living one day at a time.

The result is that we walk backward, facing forward but staring into a rearview mirror. We end up imagining a glorious past that never existed, a time of peace we never lived, a moment of security and justice that never came to pass.

We conjure paranoias. We divide the population between those dwelling in yesterday and the vanguards of tomorrow. We become zombies.

In the face of crises, simple solutions appear, born from the fertile imagination of a few unwary souls intent on breaking with the present and returning to a past consigned to history. These solutions, untethered from truth, distort, deceive, and delude. They bring more misery and chaos. In the end, exceptions do not bring solutions.
