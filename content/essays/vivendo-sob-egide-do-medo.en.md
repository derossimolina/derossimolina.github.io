There is a rap group in Brazil called O Rappa. Unlike songs filled with ostentation, sports cars, and women in bikinis, O Rappa's music reveals the harsh face of what they call, in one of their tracks, the "everyday grind." This grind crushes and pushes aside everyone who does not belong to the "system" --- that is, everyone who does not fit the narrative of the privileged layers of society.

But this grind has reached every corner of social life: at work, you must stay silent because your boss supports the government; at the university, your freedom of expression becomes a crime headline; among neighbors, you squint your eyes while forcing a smile beneath the mask that persists in the face of anti-science denialism. Nothing escapes the everyday grind --- except fear. From fear, no one is spared, because it fills the entire fabric of so-called normalcy.

You cannot criticize the dominant ideology without risking the loss of a job, a position, a scholarship, an opportunity. You must measure your words, guard your statements, your photos, your posts. I have friends who gave up resisting and turned to posting pictures of cats and dogs as a form of protest. Others gave up on the country altogether and moved to another land where they could live freely.

Yet there are those who resist the oppression of this ultra-conservative ideological model. They suffer persecution, cuts, boycotts, dismissals. They fight, struggle, swim against the current. But it wears you down and it hurts. Being among the resistance carries a bitter price, because the other "side" is relentless --- it wants only subservience and accepts no criticism. It prefers deafening silences to whispers of persistence.

If you are Black, poor, a woman, gay, or from the outskirts, then you are worse off still. Your struggle has been with you since birth; it runs in your blood. There is no bending to the whims of the powerful. When the fight becomes too heavy a burden, resignation becomes the escape. Many live one day after another with an empty stomach, an empty refrigerator, an empty soul.

There are those who deny their fear and go nowhere, becoming just another lost soul amid the crowd of simulated caricatures of social beings inhabiting the world. They live one day at a time, one step at a time, heading toward existential and moral nothingness. These people are not resigned --- they are complicit with the situation. They would rather block out the sun and endure the searing heat it brings.

Every now and then they snap out of this trance and say something potentially dangerous, but they quickly return to their catatonic state of apathy. Apathy becomes comfort, and comfort becomes a bed where they will lie, gazing at "the blue sea, the deep sky," despite their ethical myopia.

In between, we live beneath the everyday weight, resisting so we do not become resigned or shortsighted. Otherwise the struggle will have been in vain, and history nothing more than a torn page hidden so no one sees it --- out of fear of reading the truth.
